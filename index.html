Savor the journey through India's culinary heritage
Ladakh

Thukpa is a traditional Ladakhi dish that is both delicious and comforting. This hearty noodle soup is made with wheat noodles, vegetables, and your choice of meat. It is seasoned with fragrant spices and herbs, creating a flavorful and soul-warming broth. Thukpa is a must-try dish that reflects the rich culinary heritage of Ladakh.

Jammu & Kashmir

Dum Aloo is a popular Kashmiri dish that combines tender baby potatoes with a rich and flavorful gravy. The potatoes are cooked in a slow-cooked aromatic tomato and yogurt-based sauce, infused with a blend of traditional Kashmiri spices. This delicacy showcases the distinct and mouthwatering flavors of Jammu and Kashmir's cuisine, perfect for indulging in a culinary adventure.

Himachal Pradesh

Chha Gosht is a traditional Himachali dish that features succulent lamb or mutton slow-cooked in a flavorful blend of herbs and spices. This authentic dish has a distinct taste and aroma that reflects the culinary heritage of Himachal Pradesh. Served with rice, roti, or Siddu, Chha Gosht is a must-try for anyone looking to indulge in the rich and aromatic flavors of Himachali cuisine.

Punjab

Chole Bhature is a beloved Punjabi dish that combines spicy chickpea curry (chole) with deep-fried bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked in a tangy and flavorful blend of spices, while the bhature is soft, fluffy, and crispy. This indulgent and satisfying dish is a true representation of Punjab's culinary heritage.

Uttarakhand

Bal Mithai is a renowned sweet treat hailing from Uttarakhand, India. It consists of small squares of khoya (milk solids) coated with sugar and adorned with white sugar balls. With its fudgy texture, caramel-like flavor, and enticing appearance, Bal Mithai delights taste buds and represents the rich culinary heritage of Uttarakhand.

Haryana

Kadhi Pakora is a popular Haryanvi dish that combines deep-fried gram flour (besan) fritters (pakoras) with a tangy and spiced yogurt-based curry. The pakoras are crisp and flavorful, while the kadhi is thick, creamy, and sour. This mouthwatering dish exemplifies the culinary legacy of Haryana with its enticing flavors and contrasting textures.

Rajasthan

Dal Baati Churma is an iconic Rajasthani dish that showcases the rich culinary heritage of the region. It comprises three elements: Dal, a flavorful lentil curry; Baati, a baked bread with a crispy exterior and soft interior; and Churma, a sweet crumbled wheat dessert. This wholesome and delectable combination offers a symphony of flavors, textures, and aromas, representing the essence of Rajasthani cuisine.

Uttar Pradesh

Aloo Tikki is a beloved street food delicacy that originates from Uttar Pradesh. It features golden and crispy potato patties seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices. These mouthwatering tikkis are shallow-fried to perfection, resulting in a crunchy exterior and a soft, flavorful interior. Served with chutneys and garnishes, Aloo Tikki is a delightful and popular snack that satisfies taste buds with its delightful textures and savory taste.

Delhi

Dahi Bhalla is a popular street food from Delhi, India. It features deep-fried lentil fritters (vadas) that are soaked in yogurt and garnished with tangy tamarind chutney, zesty mint chutney, and a sprinkle of spices. The combination of creamy yogurt, crispy vadas, and flavorful chutneys creates a refreshing and indulgent taste experience that is loved by food enthusiasts.

Gujarat

Dhokla is a quintessential Gujarati delicacy, celebrated for its fluffy texture and delightful tanginess. Prepared from fermented chickpea flour (besan), it undergoes a steaming process before being adorned with a tempering of mustard seeds, curry leaves, and fresh coriander. This light and flavorful snack embodies the vibrant culinary traditions of Gujarat, cherished as a popular choice for its wholesome taste and nutritional value.

Madhya Pradesh

Poha is a beloved breakfast dish from Madhya Pradesh. It features flattened rice flakes cooked with a medley of spices, onions, and aromatic curry leaves. This flavorful and nourishing dish is garnished with fresh coriander and served with a side of crispy sev and a dash of tangy lemon. Poha is a popular choice for its simplicity, comforting flavors, and wholesome start to the day.

Bihar

Litti Chokha is a traditional Bihari specialty that tantalizes taste buds with its rustic flavors. It comprises roasted wheat flour balls (litti) stuffed with a delectable mix of roasted gram flour, spices, and ghee. Served with mashed potato and smoky eggplant (chokha), this hearty dish offers a delightful blend of textures and a burst of authentic flavors, representing the vibrant culinary heritage of Bihar.

Sikkim

Sikkimese Momos are a cherished specialty that captivates taste buds with their irresistible flavors. These steamed or fried dumplings feature a delicate yet chewy exterior, enveloping a tantalizing filling of vegetables, meat, or cheese. Served with a tangy tomato chutney or spicy dipping sauce, Sikkimese Momos are a quintessential street food delight that showcases the rich culinary heritage of the region.

Arunachal Pradesh

Zan, a revered dish from Arunachal Pradesh, embodies the essence of the region's culinary traditions. This wholesome porridge is crafted by simmering millets or maize flour with water to achieve a thick and comforting consistency. Often accompanied by vegetables and spices, Zan is a nourishing delight that represents the cultural richness and nutritional heritage of Arunachal Pradesh.

Assam

Assam Cha, also known as Assam Tea, is a distinguished beverage that represents the soul of Assam. Grown in the lush tea gardens of the region, it boasts a robust, malty flavor with a deep amber hue. Sipping on Assam Cha offers a delightful experience, where the rich aroma and invigorating taste transport you to the enchanting tea estates of Assam.

Meghalaya

Jadoh, a culinary gem from Meghalaya, is a delightful rice dish that tantalizes the taste buds with its aromatic flavors. Combining fragrant rice with succulent meat, such as pork or chicken, and a medley of spices, Jadoh offers a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. This beloved Khasi specialty reflects the rich cultural heritage of Meghalaya and is cherished for its comforting and satisfying nature.

Nagaland

Naga Pork Curry is a tantalizing dish from Nagaland, bursting with robust flavors and fiery spices. Succulent chunks of pork are marinated with local spices and cooked with bamboo shoots, ghost peppers, and fermented soybean paste. This aromatic and spicy delicacy embodies the essence of Naga cuisine, delighting taste buds and offering a memorable gastronomic experience.

Manipur

Eromba, a traditional Manipuri dish, is a delightful fusion of boiled vegetables, fermented fish, and aromatic spices. This zesty delicacy showcases the vibrant flavors of Manipuri cuisine, with a tangy and savory profile that awakens the palate. Eromba is a culinary masterpiece, representing the rich cultural heritage of Manipur and offering a unique and unforgettable dining experience.

Tripura

Bai, a culinary treasure from Tripura, mesmerizes with its delightful flavors and aromatic spices. This traditional fish stew showcases the region's love for seafood, with succulent fish simmered in a flavorful broth enriched with herbs and fermented bamboo shoots. Bai is a spicy and tangy delicacy that embodies the rich cultural heritage and culinary traditions of Tripura.

Mizoram

Misa Mach Poora, a delectable dish from Mizoram, tantalizes the taste buds with its unique flavors. Grilled shrimps are marinated with local spices and cooked to perfection, resulting in a delightful seafood delicacy. Misa Mach Poora showcases the rich culinary heritage of Mizoram, offering a harmonious blend of smoky, savory, and aromatic notes that leave a lasting impression.

Jharkhand

Dhuska, a beloved culinary delight from Jharkhand, is a crispy and savory snack that captivates taste buds. Made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils, these deep-fried discs boast a golden brown hue and a delightful crunch. Served with aromatic aloo curry or chutney, Dhuska embodies the rich flavors and cultural traditions of Jharkhand, offering a memorable culinary experience.

West Bengal

Rosogolla, an iconic Bengali sweet, is a heavenly delicacy that brings joy to every palate. These spongy white balls of delight are made from soft paneer dough, cooked to perfection, and soaked in a sugar syrup infused with aromatic flavors. Rosogolla is a timeless symbol of West Bengal's rich culinary heritage and is cherished as a symbol of love and celebration.

Maharashtra

Vada Pav, an iconic street food of Maharashtra, is a delectable combination of flavors and textures. A spiced potato fritter is encased in a soft bun, accompanied by zesty chutneys and aromatic spices. This savory delight captures the essence of Maharashtra's culinary heritage, offering a burst of flavors that leaves a lasting impression. Vada Pav is a beloved snack that embodies the spirit of Mumbai's bustling street food scene.

Chhattisgarh

Chila, a culinary gem from Chhattisgarh, is a delightful and versatile dish that captivates taste buds. Made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils, these thin and savory pancakes offer a soft and fluffy texture. Served with chutney or curry, Chila is a beloved breakfast or snack option that showcases the traditional flavors and cultural heritage of Chhattisgarh.

Odisha

Pakhala, a culinary treasure of Odisha, is a nourishing and refreshing dish that tantalizes taste buds. Cooked rice is soaked in water and left to ferment overnight, resulting in a delightful tangy flavor. Served with an array of accompaniments like fried fish, curries, and vegetables, Pakhala offers a unique culinary experience that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Odisha.

Telangana

Hyderabadi Biryani is a popular rice dish originating from the Indian city of Hyderabad. The dish is made with long grain basmati rice and flavored with aromatic spices, herbs, and saffron. It is typically layered with marinated meat (chicken or lamb) and cooked on a slow flame, resulting in a fragrant and spicy dish with tender meat. Served with raita or salan, it is a favorite among food lovers worldwide.

Karnataka

Masala Dosa, a beloved culinary delight from Karnataka, is a crispy rice and lentil crepe stuffed with a spicy and flavorful potato filling. This South Indian delicacy offers a perfect balance of textures and tastes, with the dosa's golden exterior and the savory filling. Served with sambar and coconut chutney, Masala Dosa is a must-try dish that showcases the rich flavors and culinary heritage of Karnataka.

Andhra Pradesh

Gongura Pachadi, a culinary gem from Andhra Pradesh, is a tangy and flavorful chutney that tantalizes taste buds. Made from the leaves of the gongura plant, it offers a unique combination of tanginess and spiciness. With its vibrant red color and distinctive taste, Gongura Pachadi is a beloved accompaniment in Andhra cuisine, adding a zesty kick to any meal.

Kerala

Appam with Stew is a signature dish from Kerala, offering a delightful culinary experience. Appam is a lacy and soft rice pancake with a slightly crispy edge, while Stew is a fragrant and creamy coconut-based curry infused with spices. The combination of these two dishes creates a harmonious blend of textures and flavors, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of Kerala.

Tamil Nadu

Idli with Sambar and Chutney is an iconic dish from Tamil Nadu, offering a delightful culinary experience. Idli is a steamed rice cake that is light, fluffy, and incredibly versatile. Served with piping hot sambar, a flavorful lentil soup with vegetables, and a variety of chutneys, such as coconut and tomato, this combination creates a perfect balance of flavors, textures, and nutrition, representing the rich culinary heritage of Tamil Nadu.
